## Digital Fog
**Definition**  
The confusion, drag, and hidden cost created when a business has too many disconnected tools, scattered workflows, unclear ownership, weak follow-up, and no operating system.

**Symptoms**  
Too many tools, no single source of truth, manual follow-up, messy handoffs, repeated decisions, unclear priorities, lost leads, slow execution, no visibility, unclear ownership, scattered files, half-built automations, duplicated work, AI tools producing more noise.

**Use**  
Top-of-funnel hook. Names the pain before the solution.

**Copy**  
- Your business does not need another AI tool. It needs less digital fog.
- Before you automate, find the fog.
- The mess is not in your tools. It is in the space between them.

## Context Architecture
**Definition**  
The process of organizing the information, workflows, decisions, assets, systems, and operating logic a business needs before AI or automation can work properly.

**Rule**  
No client starts infrastructure, automation, marketing system, workflow system, or agent work without Context Architecture.

**Stages**  
1. Signal Capture  
2. Context Mapping  
3. Architecture Design  
4. Operational Baseline  
5. Implementation Roadmap

**Primary Artifact**  
Context Architecture Blueprint (business map, system map, workflow map, tool map, lead flow map, content map, revenue map, automation opportunities, risk points, decision rules, roadmap, acceptance criteria).

**Public Line**  
AI cannot help if it does not know where the truth lives.

## WIN
**What's Important Now**  
Dual layer:  
- Workstream Impact Node: persistent memory. Captures what happened, why, what changed, what to remember, what to improve.  
- Workstream Intellect Nexus: recursive learning. Turns every cycle into better routing, prompts, envelopes, standards, decisions.

**Risk-Tiered Routing** (core of the product)  
- Reputation risk → Alpha (voice / content / strategy)  
- Time-loss risk → Beta (ops / automation / coordination)  
- Downtime / security / money / production risk → Gamma (two-pass, diff, approval, rollback)

## FLOW Agent AS (FAAS)
**Frictionless Leveraged Orchestrated Workflow — Agent Architected Schemas**  
Execution governance for AI-assisted work.

**What it does**  
- Task enters as structured envelope  
- Validates  
- Routes by risk (Alpha/Beta/Gamma)  
- Executes or escalates  
- Writes artifacts  
- Captures audit  
- Reflects to WIN

**Three-Agent Model**  
- Alpha: reputation-risk (copy, briefs, comms, strategy) — no secrets, code, infra, prod.  
- Beta: time-loss risk (automation, scheduling, ops) — escalates on boundary cross.  
- Gamma: downtime/security/money risk — two-pass only: draft, review, diff, approval, rollback plan.

**Core Rule**  
FLOW routes by risk, not personality. "What is the limiting factor if this goes wrong?"

**What it is not**  
Not Vercel. Not Cursor. Not Make/Zapier replacement. Not autonomous workforce. Not enterprise-ready yet.  
It is the control layer that makes those platforms safer.

**Positioning**  
Like Intel inside the machine: the trusted execution governance layer for AI-assisted operations.

Structure the Work
